About Indian Red Cross Society

A legacy of humanitarian service connecting global compassion with local action

Origin and Global Foundation

The Red Cross Movement was founded in 1863 by Jean Henri Dunant, a Swiss humanitarian who witnessed the suffering of wounded soldiers at the Battle of Solferino. His vision was to create an organization that would provide impartial care to the wounded during wartime.

This spirit of compassion echoes the humanitarian work of Bhai Ghanaiya, who served wounded soldiers without discrimination over 300 years ago in Punjab, embodying the same principles of universal service to humanity.

Indian Red Cross Society

The Indian Red Cross Society was established in 1920 and is one of the largest humanitarian organizations in India. The Punjab State Red Cross Branch was formed in 1925 in Lahore, and after partition, a separate Punjab State Red Cross Branch was established in 1971.

The Ludhiana District Branch has been serving the community with dedication, providing essential services in health, welfare, and emergency relief.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Red Cross membership?

Membership is a formal affiliation with the Indian Red Cross Society, giving you the opportunity to support and participate in humanitarian activities.

Who can become a member?

Any individual or organization committed to humanitarian service can become a member. Age restrictions apply only to Student Membership (15-25 years).

How long is membership valid?

Life Membership is valid for lifetime. Annual and Student Memberships are valid for one year and can be renewed. Institutional Membership is annual.

Can membership be transferred?

Membership is non-transferable and is registered in the name of the individual or organization.

What if I want to upgrade my membership?

You can upgrade from Annual to Life Membership by paying the difference amount. Contact our office for details.

Can I cancel my membership?

Life Membership is non-refundable. Annual memberships can be cancelled but the fee is non-refundable.

Are there age restrictions?

Student Membership is for ages 15-25. Other membership categories have no age restrictions.

Do I get a receipt for tax filing?

Yes, membership fees qualify for tax exemption under Section 80G. You will receive an official receipt.

Pangoora Ghar (Baby Rescue)

Saving abandoned newborn babies since 2010

Overview

Established on March 25, 2010, Pangoora Ghar is a dedicated facility for rescuing and caring for abandoned newborn babies. To date, we have saved 15 precious lives, providing them with safety, medical care, and hope for a better future.

Purpose and Mission

Our mission is to provide a safe haven for abandoned babies, ensuring they receive immediate medical attention, proper nutrition, and loving care. We work to secure their future through legal adoption processes and long-term care arrangements.

How the Facility Operates

Pangoora Ghar operates 24/7 with trained staff and volunteers. Any abandoned baby found can be brought to our facility where immediate care is provided. We maintain strict confidentiality and follow all legal procedures.

Care Provided

  • üîπ 24/7 medical care and monitoring
  • üîπ Proper nutrition and feeding
  • üîπ Safe and hygienic housing
  • üîπ Regular health monitoring
  • üîπ Future planning and adoption assistance

Process

1. Emergency Rescue
Immediate response to reports
2. Medical Examination
Comprehensive health check
3. Care & Rehabilitation
Daily care and monitoring
4. Legal Processing
Documentation and procedures
5. Adoption Facilitation
Finding loving homes

Legal and Ethical Framework

Pangoora Ghar operates under child protection laws and follows all guidelines set by child welfare authorities. We maintain complete confidentiality and ensure that all babies receive legal identity documents and proper care.

Adoption Information

We work with authorized adoption agencies to find loving, caring families for the babies in our care. If you're interested in adoption, please contact us for guidance on the legal adoption process.

Success Stories

Of the 15 babies we have saved, many have been successfully placed in loving homes through legal adoption. These children now have the opportunity for a bright future with families who cherish them.

You can support Pangoora Ghar through monetary donations, baby care supplies (diapers, formula, clothes), or by volunteering your time. All donations are eligible for tax exemption under Section 80G.
